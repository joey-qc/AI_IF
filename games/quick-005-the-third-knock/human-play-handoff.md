## Player-Facing Setup

## Opening Scene

The seance parlor at Harrowmere House is narrow, candlelit, and over-furnished, its velvet curtains drawn tight against the night. A round table sits at the center beneath a shaded oil lamp. On it rest a spirit bell, a planchette board, and four pairs of nervous hands.

Beatrice Hallow sits nearest the curtains, pale but composed. Around her are Clara Vane, the professional medium; Edmund Hallow, Beatrice's tense nephew; and Lydia Harrow, Beatrice's quiet companion and secretary.

The door is locked from the inside at Beatrice's insistence.

"The dead are not the only ones who have spoken for me," Beatrice says.

The lamp is lowered. The first knock sounds. Then a second. On the third, Beatrice gives a strained gasp and slumps in her chair.

When the lamp is raised, no one has entered. No one has left. The three suspects stare at one another across the seance table.

The case begins.

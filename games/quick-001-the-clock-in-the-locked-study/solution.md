# Solution: The Clock in the Locked Study

## Case Identity

- Case ID: `quick-001`
- Title: `The Clock in the Locked Study`
- Status: Draft, not yet validated

## Central Mystery

Rowan Vale's rare Breguet pocket watch vanished from a locked glass display case during a small gathering in his study. The room remained closed, and only three people were present:

- Rowan Vale, the owner;
- Celia Vale, his niece;
- Thomas Greer, his valet.

The player must determine who took the watch, how it was removed, and where it is hidden.

## Culprit

Thomas Greer, Rowan Vale's valet.

## Motive

Thomas had a gambling debt due within two days. If exposed, he would likely lose his position and social standing. The watch was valuable enough to solve the immediate debt problem.

This motive is proportional because the central crime is theft, not murder. Thomas takes a serious risk, but not an irrational one: he expects suspicion to fall on Celia because of her pawnshop envelope.

## Method

Thomas did not open the front lock of the display case.

Earlier that afternoon, while polishing the study, he loosened the rear hinge screws of the display case. During the evening gathering, while moving naturally between the tea trolley, display case, and clock, he lifted the loosened rear edge just enough to remove the watch.

He then hid the watch inside the tallcase clock's pendulum compartment.

## Opportunity

Thomas was in the room during the gathering. His duties allowed him to move around the study without seeming suspicious. He had normal access to the display case earlier in the day and normal access to the clock because he wound and maintained it.

## Concealment

Thomas intended to retrieve the watch later, after suspicion settled on Celia.

His concealment plan depended on three things:

1. The front lock appeared untouched.
2. Celia looked suspicious because of her pawnshop envelope.
3. The clock seemed like ordinary furniture rather than a hiding place.

## Culprit Mistakes

Thomas made several mistakes:

1. He left the rear hinge slightly loose.
2. He left fresh brass dust below the hinge.
3. He snagged black livery thread on the hinge.
4. He transferred clock oil to the hinge screw.
5. He jammed the watch inside the clock, stopping it at 8:13.
6. He mentioned the stopped clock too early while trying to defend himself.

## Essential Clues

| Clue | Meaning | Resolution |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Loose rear hinge | The watch did not leave through the front lock | Thomas loosened the hinge earlier |
| Fresh brass dust | The hinge was disturbed recently | Proves recent tampering |
| Black wool thread | The hinge caught Thomas's livery thread | Links Thomas to the display case |
| Clock oil on hinge screw | The person who touched the hinge also handled clock oil | Points to Thomas's clock duties |
| Clock stopped at 8:13 | Something interfered with the clock mechanism | The hidden watch stopped the clock |
| Fresh clock latch scrape | The clock compartment was opened recently | Thomas opened it to hide the watch |
| Faint ticking in the clock | The watch is still running inside the stopped clock | The watch is recovered there |
| Thomas's premature clock comment | Thomas is too aware of the clock problem | He knows because he used the clock as hiding place |

## Red Herring

### Celia's pawnshop envelope

The envelope appears to suggest Celia needed money and may have stolen the watch.

Truth: Celia pawned her own pearl bracelet to pay art-school fees. She is embarrassed, but innocent.

The pawn ticket can clear her because it names a bracelet, not the watch.

## Final Reveal

A proper final reveal should cover these points:

1. The front lock was never defeated.
2. The rear hinge was loosened before the gathering.
3. The fresh brass dust proves recent tampering.
4. The black thread came from Thomas's livery.
5. The oil on the hinge matches Thomas's clock oil.
6. The stopped clock points to the hiding place.
7. The watch is found inside the clock's pendulum compartment.
8. Celia's pawnshop envelope explains her nervousness but not the theft.
9. Thomas stole the watch to pay a gambling debt and planned to retrieve it later.

## Required Proof Threshold

A correct accusation should include at least:

- the loose rear hinge;
- the black thread;
- the clock oil;
- the stopped clock;
- the watch found inside the clock.

The player may suspect Thomas before finding the watch, but the accusation is strongest only when the hidden watch is discovered.

## Post-Resolution Answers

### Who took the watch?

Thomas Greer.

### Why?

He needed money quickly to pay a gambling debt and avoid disgrace.

### How?

He loosened the rear hinge of the display case before the gathering and removed the watch through the back edge while serving tea.

### Where was the watch?

Inside the tallcase clock's pendulum compartment.

### Was Celia involved?

No. Her pawnshop envelope was embarrassing but unrelated. It concerned her own bracelet.
